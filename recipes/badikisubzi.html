Badi Ki subzi

Description

This recipe uses simple Kumaoni flavors with mustard oil and minimal spices, reflecting the rustic cuisine of Uttarakhand. Badi Ki Subzi is nutritious and a staple in Kumaoni households

Ingredients

  • 10-12 dried Arbi (colocasia) badi (sun-dried lentil dumplings)
  • 6-7 tbsp mustard oil (or as needed)
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp asafoetida (hing)
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped onion (optional)
  • 1/3 cup grated or chopped tomatoes
  • 1 large potato, peeled and grated
  • 1 tbsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tbsp chickpea flour (besan) mixed with 4 tbsp water (for thickening)
  • Water as needed
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnishing

Steps

  1. Heat 3 tablespoons mustard oil in a pan on low flame. Add the dried badi and roast them until lightly browned and cooked inside. Remove and keep aside.
  2. In the same pan, heat the remaining mustard oil, add cumin seeds and asafoetida.
  3. Optionally add chopped onions and sauté until light brown
  4. Add grated tomatoes and cook until the oil separates from the masala.
  5. Mix in turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Cook for 2-3 minutes.
  6. Add grated potato and sauté for a few minutes.
  7. Add water (about 1 cup), bring to a gentle boil.
  8. Add the roasted badi into the gravy.
  9. Stir in the chickpea flour-water mix gradually to thicken the curry.
  10. Simmer on low flame for 10-15 minutes until the curry thickens and badi absorb the flavors.
  11. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with mandua roti or steamed rice.
